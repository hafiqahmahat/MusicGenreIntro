Type of Genre


Genre Example Song Element
Classical Ludwig Van Beethoven
Moonlight Sonata
  • An emphasis on elegance and balance.
  • Short well-balanced melodies and
    clear-cut question and answer phrases.
  • Mainly simple diatonic harmony.
  • Mainly homophonic textures (melody plus accompaniment) but with some use of counterpoint(where two or
    more melodic lines are combined).
  • Use of contrasting moods.
Jazz Tommy Dorsey and His Orchestra
Sweet Sue, Just You
  • Heavy on instrumental
  • Improvisation
  • Swing and syncopation
  • Bent notes and modes
  • Distinctive voices
Opera Maria Callas
Casta Diva! - Norma
  • Singers and musicians perform a dramatic work combining text (libretto) and musical score.
  • Usually in a theatrical setting.
  • Opera incorporates many of the elements of spoken theatre, such as acting,scenery, and costumes.
  • Sometimes includes dance.
Rock n Roll Elvis Presley
Jailhouse Rock
  • Rhythm and blues, then called "race music", and country music of the 1940s and 1950s.
  • Particularly significant influences were jazz, blues, gospel, country, and folk.
Rock Joan Jett and The Blackhearts
I Love Rock 'n' Roll
  • Originated as "rock and roll".
  • Rock has centered on the electric guitar.
  • Usually as part of a rock group with electric bass, drums, and one or more singers.
  • Song-based music usually with a 4/4 time signature using a verse–chorus form.
Hip-Hop The Notorious B.I.G
Hypnotize
  • A stylized rhythmic music that commonly accompanies rapping, a rhythmic and rhyming speech that is chanted.
  • A subculture defined by four key stylistic elements: MCing/rapping, DJing/scratching with turntables, break dancing, and graffiti writing.
  • Other elements include sampling beats or bass lines from records (or synthesized beats and sounds), and rhythmic beatboxing.
R&B Destiny's Child
Say My Name
  • A music genre that combines elements of rhythm and blues, pop, soul, funk, hip hop and electronic music.
  • Features a distinctive record production style, drum machine-backed rhythms, pitch corrected vocals, and a smooth, lush style of vocal arrangement.
Raggae Bob Marley
No Women No Cry
  • Incorporates some of the musical elements of rhythm and blues (R&B), jazz, mento, calypso, African, and Latin American music, as well as other genres.
  • Reggae scenes consist of two guitars, one for rhythm and one for lead—drums, congas, and keyboards, with a couple vocalists.
Ballad Whitney Houston
I will Always Love You
  • A form of verse, often a narrative set to music.
  • A ballad can be a slow, mournful love song—but it can also be a silly, light poem.
  • Slow and emotionally evocative.
Pop Black Eyed Peas
I Gotta Feeling
  • A riff is a short, repeated melodic pattern, often forming the background to a solo or vocal line.
  • It is usually one to four bars long.
  • A hook is a short catchy melodic idea designed to be instantly memorable.
Electronic Dance Music (EDM) Skrillex
Bangarang
  • EDM music pieces have a relatively high and steady tempo. In most cases it is around 129-150 beats per minute (BPM).
  • A broad range of percussive electronic music genres made largely for nightclubs, raves and festivals.
  • Most EDM tracks can be broken down into four main structural elements: Intro, Breakdown, Build up, Drop.
Korean Pop (K-pop) BTS
Dope
  • Originated from South Korea
  • A musical genre consisting of electronic, hip hop, pop, rock, and R&B music.
